Bhaktapur Durbar Square

A mesmerizing UNESCO World Heritage Site that showcases the rich cultural heritage of Nepal.

About Bhaktapur Durbar Square

Bhaktapur Durbar Square is one of the most remarkable architectural masterpieces of Nepal. Located in the ancient city of Bhaktapur, the square is a treasure trove of palaces, temples, and traditional Newari craftsmanship.

Key Attractions

  • **55 Window Palace**: A masterpiece of woodcarving built during the reign of King Yaksha Malla.
  • **Nyatapola Temple**: Nepal’s tallest pagoda, dedicated to Siddhi Lakshmi, the goddess of prosperity.
  • **Golden Gate**: An intricately designed gateway leading to the palace courtyard.
  • **Pottery Square**: Witness the age-old tradition of pottery making by local artisans.

Historical Significance

The square reflects Nepal's medieval history and its importance during the Malla Dynasty. The rich cultural and religious practices have made Bhaktapur Durbar Square a living museum, where every corner tells a story.

Tips for Visitors

  • Visit early in the morning to enjoy the serene ambiance.
  • Hire a local guide to fully understand the historical significance.
  • Try the famous **"Juju Dhau"** (King of Yogurt), a local delicacy.
